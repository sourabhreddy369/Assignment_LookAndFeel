  • Models Implemented by Schools

    • Since President Obama took office, Congress has appropriated more than $4 billion to help turn around the nation's lowest-performing schools. States were awarded nearly $3.5 billion in School Improvement Grant funds in 2010 to turn around their persistently lowest achieving schools. School districts then applied to state for the funds this spring. When school districts applied, they were required to indicate that they would implement one of the following four models in their persistently lowest achieving schools

    On December 3, 2009, U.S. Secretary of Education Arne Duncan announced the final requirements for $3.5 billion in Title I School Improvement Grants (SIGs) to turn around the nation’s lowest performing schools. The applications were due into the U.S. Department of Education by Feb. 8, 2010. These funds were made available to states by formula and competed for by school districts. As they competed for the funds, school districts/local educational agencies (LEAs) were required to identify the schools they wanted to transform, and then determine which of four models was most appropriate: the turnaround model, the restart model, school closure, or the transformation model. The law delineates the requirements for each of the four models. If a school had begun implementation of one of these four models or components of one of these models within the previous two years, it was permitted to apply to use SIG funds to continue to implement the full model.

  • 1. Turnaround Model

    • If a school is ready to implement "Turnaround Model" to receive funds, then it has to replace the principal, screen existing school staff, and rehire no more than half the teachers; adopt a new governance structure; and improve the school through curriculum reform, professional development, extending learning time, and other strategies.

    There are 165 schools in total across different states that are ready to implement Turnaround Model. Out of them California state has highest number of schools - 28, and South Dakota, Nebraska, New Mexico have only 1 school that is ready to implement the turnaround model.

    All the schools in Turnaround Model are granted a total of around $65 million funds and with an average of $394 thousand.

  • 2. Restart Model

    • If a school is ready to implement "Restart Model" to receive funds, then it has to convert a school or close it and re-open it as a charter school or under an education management organization.

    There are 33 schools in total across different states that are ready to implement Restart Model. Out of them Pennsylvania state has highest number of schools - 7, and 6 states (Alaska, Colorado, Connecticut, Illinois, North Carolina, New Jersey) have only 1 school that is ready to implement the Restart model.

    All the schools in Restart Model are granted a total of around $16 million funds and with an average of $503 thousand.

  • 3. School Closure Model

    • If a school is ready to implement "Closure Model" to receive funds, then it has to close the school and send the students to higher-achieving schools in the district.

    There are 168 schools in total across different states that are ready to implement Turnaround Model. Out of them Wisconsin state has highest number of schools - 4, and South Carolina, Washington and Missouri have only 1 school that is ready to implement the Closure model.

    All the schools in Closure Model are granted a total of around $1 million funds and with an average of $75 thousand.

  • 4. Transformation Model

    • If a school is ready to implement "Transformation Model" to receive funds, then it has to replace the principal and improve the school through comprehensive curriculum reform, professional development, extending learning time, and other strategies.

    There are 543 schools in total across different states that are ready to implement Transformation Model. Out of them California state has highest number of schools - 56, and North Dakota have only 1 school that is ready to implement the Transformation model.

    All the schools in Transformation Model are granted a total of around $235 million funds and with an average of $434 thousand.
